# 👥 IBM HR Analytics Dashboard

## Employee Attrition & Workforce Insights using Power BI

An interactive Power BI dashboard designed to analyze employee attrition patterns, workforce demographics, job satisfaction, compensation trends, and workplace factors that influence employee retention. The dashboard transforms HR data into actionable insights that can help organizations improve employee engagement and reduce turnover.

---

# 📌 Short Description

The IBM HR Analytics Dashboard is a comprehensive HR reporting solution built in Power BI using the IBM Employee Attrition dataset. It enables HR professionals, business leaders, and analysts to identify the key drivers of employee attrition and understand how factors such as age, income, overtime, work-life balance, and job satisfaction impact workforce retention.

The dashboard provides an interactive environment for exploring employee trends and uncovering insights that support data-driven HR decision-making.

---

# 🛠 Tech Stack

The dashboard was built using the following tools and technologies:

• 📊 **Power BI Desktop** – Main data visualization platform used to create the interactive dashboard.

• 📂 **Power Query** – Used for data cleaning, transformation, and preparation before analysis.

• 🧠 **DAX (Data Analysis Expressions)** – Used to create KPIs, calculated measures, attrition metrics, and dynamic business calculations.

• 🔗 **Data Modeling** – Relationships and optimized data structures used to enable accurate reporting and filtering.

• 🎨 **Dashboard Design Principles** – Applied to create a clean, user-friendly, and business-focused reporting experience.

• 📁 **File Formats**

* `.pbix` for dashboard development
* `.csv` for source data
* `.pdf` for dashboard export and documentation

# ⭐ Features & Highlights

## Business Problem

Employee attrition is one of the most significant challenges faced by organizations today. High turnover increases recruitment costs, reduces productivity, affects team morale, and results in the loss of valuable organizational knowledge.

HR leaders often struggle to answer questions such as:

* Which employee groups are most likely to leave?
* Does overtime increase employee turnover?
* Which departments experience the highest attrition?
* How does job satisfaction impact retention?
* Are compensation levels influencing employee exits?
* Which demographic segments require retention strategies?

Without a centralized analytical solution, identifying these patterns can be difficult and time-consuming.

---

## Goal of the Dashboard

The dashboard was designed to:

* Monitor employee attrition across the organization.
* Identify demographic and behavioral factors associated with employee turnover.
* Analyze employee satisfaction and work-life balance trends.
* Support HR teams in designing targeted retention initiatives.
* Provide leadership with actionable workforce insights.

# 📊 Walkthrough of Key Visuals

## 1️⃣ Attrition Overview Dashboard

### Key Performance Indicators

The top section provides a high-level overview of workforce statistics:

* Total Employees: 1,470
* Active Employees: 1,233
* Attrition Count: 237
* Attrition Rate: 16.12%

These KPIs allow stakeholders to quickly assess the organization's workforce health.

### Attrition by Gender

A donut chart visualizes employee attrition by gender:

* Male: 63%
* Female: 37%

This helps identify gender-based attrition trends.

### Attrition by Department

A departmental comparison highlights where employee turnover is most concentrated:

* Research & Development
* Sales
* Human Resources

This enables department-level retention planning.

### Attrition by Job Role

A ranked visualization identifies job roles with the highest turnover, including:

* Laboratory Technician
* Sales Executive
* Research Scientist
* Sales Representative

This helps HR teams focus retention efforts on critical positions.

---

## 2️⃣ Employee Demographics Dashboard

### Attrition by Age Group

Employees are segmented into age categories to identify vulnerable workforce segments.

Key Finding:

* Employees aged 25–34 show the highest attrition levels.

### Attrition by Marital Status

The dashboard compares turnover among:

* Single Employees
* Married Employees
* Divorced Employees

Key Finding:

* Single employees have the highest attrition rate.

### Attrition by Education Field

Employees are analyzed based on educational background:

* Life Sciences
* Medical
* Marketing
* Technical Degree
* Human Resources
* Other

This helps identify educational segments with higher turnover.

### Monthly Income Analysis

A comparison of average monthly income between employees who stayed and employees who left.

This visual helps determine whether compensation may be influencing attrition decisions.

---

## 3️⃣ Satisfaction & Work Factors Dashboard

### Attrition by Overtime

Employees are segmented based on overtime status.

Key Finding:

* Employees working overtime show significantly higher attrition.

### Job Satisfaction Analysis

A matrix visual compares satisfaction levels across different job roles.

Satisfaction categories include:

* Low
* Medium
* High
* Very High

This helps identify areas where employee engagement initiatives may be needed.

### Attrition by Work-Life Balance

Employees are grouped based on their work-life balance ratings.

This visual helps determine whether workplace flexibility and employee well-being influence retention.

### Attrition by Years at Company

A tenure analysis examines employee exits across different experience levels.

This helps HR understand whether attrition is concentrated among new hires or long-term employees.
